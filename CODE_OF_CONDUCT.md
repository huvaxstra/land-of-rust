# 🛡️ Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in **The Land of Rust** project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community – especially because this content is designed for **children and young learners**.

## 🌱 Special Commitments for Youth-Focused Content

Because this book is intended for ages 9+, we additionally commit to:

✅ Using **age-appropriate, encouraging language** in all examples and explanations  
✅ Framing errors and debugging as **normal, positive parts of learning**  
✅ Avoiding metaphors or scenarios that could cause anxiety or confusion  
✅ Prioritizing **emotional safety** alongside technical accuracy  
✅ Welcoming feedback from parents, teachers, and young readers  

## 🚫 Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional, youth-friendly setting

## 📬 Reporting & Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainers at: **[your email]**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

## 🌐 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub, Zulip, email), and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces (e.g., speaking at an event, posting on social media as a maintainer).
